About Me!

Hello! My name is Hannah Hamilton and I'm 19 years old. I'm a student at San Jacinto College and getting my associate's degree. I have made this website to show my digital artwork. As I continue to make art, I will add more of my pieces here! This website will tell you a little bit about me, my art journey, and will show how I get my creativety out in digital form.

My Art Journey.

My art journey started when I was really little. I've been drawing ever since I was a little girl. I loved it! There was always paper in my house somewhere so, I was always drawing. Fun fact! When I was younger I absolutely loved drawing with ink pens. I rarely did drawings with pencils. It was always ink pens for me. I used to draw anything and everything. Dogs, cats, lions, tigers, mermaids, sharks, dragons, and much more! I can see myself now sitting down at my desk making drawing, after drawing, after drawing. I could do 3-5 drawings a day. Sometimes more! It's always been a part of me. I've been a traditional artist for years. Now that I am older I'm exploring another art form. Digital art!

I do all of my digital art in Adobe Photoshop. My first time using this program was during my first semester in my computer graphics class. I had a really good time using it and got to use it again in two of my classes during my second semester at college. I got to use it a lot for my assignments and now I use it in my free time to make art. I most of my digital artwork I post on my Instagram. I've posted traditional and digital art there. Over these last two semesters at college I've grown to really enjoy being a digital artist. This is something I want to continue to do for a long time. I'll always make art in some way or form because it's a part of me. A part of me a never want to let go.

"The Land Beyond"

The first artwork I have to show is this one here. This piece is called "The Land Beyond". "The Land Beyond" is one of two pieces that I had in the 2026 Student Art Gallery Show on campus.This artwork is one of my favorites that I have done. This actually one of my works for my Iron Desktop assignment that I had in my Introduction to Digital Imaging class. My professor sent the class a folder with pictures in it. Our task was to make an image from at least five of the pictures in the folder. We could do whatever we wanted as long as we did our work in Photoshop. We had to make at least three images this way. When making this one I had a pretty good idea of what I wanted to do. I wanted to make a place that doesn't exist from a place that exists. With a mysterious vibe. Almost everything you see in this work came from an image. The mountain on the right side came from another image. The white orbs came from another image. All of the pictures I used, I was able to manipulate them how I needed to so my final result would look the way I wanted it to. How many images do you think I used in this artwork?

"Forest Watchers"

The is the second artwork I had in the 2026 Student Art Gallery Show. "Forest Watchers" is not from a class assignment, but something I did on my own at home for the show. I love this piece so much. I had a lot of fun with this one in Photoshop! I love how I used masking, blending modes, and brighten and contrast to get the look that I wanted. While this piece was in the gallery, I had a few people tell me that this made them think of the Twilight movie covers. I didn't make this with Twilight in mind. However, after looking at my work I could see why some people thought that. How many animals do you see here?
